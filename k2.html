Postnatal Depression


(also known as PPD)

This Depression that occurs after childbirth. Those who develop postpartum depression are at greater risk of developing major depression later on in life. Symptoms might include insomnia, loss of appetite, intense irritability and difficulty bonding with the baby. Untreated, the condition may last months or longer. Treatment can include counselling, antidepressants or hormone therapy.This is very common depression and more than 10 million cases found per year in India.
Requires a medical diagnosis
Symptoms might include insomnia, loss of appetite, intense irritability and difficulty bonding with the baby.

People may experience:
Mood: anger, anxiety, guilt, hopelessness, loss of interest or pleasure in activities, mood swings, or panic attack
Behavioural: crying, irritability, or restlessness
Whole body: fatigue or loss of appetite
Weight: weight gain or weight loss
Cognitive: lack of concentration or unwanted thoughts
Psychological: depression or fear
Also common: insomnia or repeatedly going over thoughts
